Finish leetcode

As it may be inferred from the introduction, I studied CS a long time ago (back in 1996). By the time I was spit out into the industry, the panorama was quite different than today; on interviews you were pretty much asked about concrete technology. "Have you used programming language X, or RDBMS Y?", were typical questions and sometimes were accompanied by practical programming exercises.

Today's panorama for a college graduate in CS is quite different; knowing concrete technologies is not the main filter, but algorithm questions are. I guess Google started the trend and nowadays every company, from the startup to the corporate dinosaurs, just follow it. The rational is that such questions measure the really important skills (problem solving), and that technologies can be learned easily. I guess that I agree with the idea, except for the fact that I studied those topics two decades ago; and never really embraced frequently that kind of questions. Because of that, along with my particular way of thinking, I found extremely challenging to solve those problems under the clock. I literally feel this is a skill that I am slowly developing throughout the years, specially because time investment has not been consistent.

I started trying on several platforms until I friend recommended me leetcode. IHMO, it has the richest set of questions and a great community to learn from; the solutions and explanations posted by some members are almost surreal, many things to learn there. My first personal project then, is to finish all leetcode questions; the almost 1500+ algorithm problems (circa 2020). It looks almost like an impossible task, specially due the hard problems, but that is precisely why I should do it. My leetcode user is de_mexico, and I backup my solutions in the gitlab repo below:

https://gitlab.com/dario.mx/topcoder-srm

Enjoy with cheese!
